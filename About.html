What is TopGear?


Topgear is a reality television show. Unless there is golf or another sport on, it airs on BBC Two on Sunday evenings at 8pm. When the episodes are finished, it is no longer broadcast on BBC Two, although it is still available on BBC iPlayer for those who were too busy watching golf or another sport in the first place.



Top Gear is a car-related television show. It provides expert consumer advise to the ardent motoring, um, enthusiast. Taking a trio of V8 muscle cars on moonshine runs, drifting the streets of London in a souped-up Ford Mustang, searching for Bigfoot with all-terrain buggies, buying cheap used Japanese coupes and attempting to drift them in tandem, climbing mountains in small city cars... you get the picture. All extremely sensible advice.

Development


After the BBC dropped the original Top Gear format in December 2001, Jeremy Clarkson and producer Andy Wilman gathered to brainstorm ideas for resurrecting the show for television. This finally led to them meeting with the broadcaster to present the notion of switching from a motoring magazine format to a studio-based approach. Among the ideas proposed were: the use of a fixed location for car reviews and other films, in addition to locations in the UK and abroad; putting notable cars through a timed lap of a circuit; the use of a test driver with veteran racing experience, who drives some of the cars for the show; and the participation of celebrity guests who would be invited to take part in an interview.



Following the pitch, the BBC opted to green-light the new format in order to establish a show to compete with Channel 5's new motoring show Fifth Gear, to which several original Top Gear hosts, including Tiff Needell, Vicki Butler-Henderson, and producer Jon Bentley, went.
